Disease Control: Protecting Yourself and Your Community

Common Diseases

Malaria

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites transmitted through the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. It's one of the most significant health challenges in South Sudan.

Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis is a bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs but can also impact other parts of the body. It's a significant health concern in South Sudan, often complicated by co-infection with HIV.

Cholera

Cholera is an acute diarrheal infection caused by ingesting food or water contaminated with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. Outbreaks in South Sudan are often associated with poor sanitation and lack of clean water.

HIV/AIDS

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) attacks the body's immune system, leading to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) if left untreated. HIV/AIDS remains a significant health challenge in South Sudan.
